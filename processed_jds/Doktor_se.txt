Own the platform for Sweden's largest digital healthcare provider. AWS, Kubernetes, AI workloads in production. Small engineering team, real influence, no red tape.

Why Doktor.se

Doktor.se is Sweden's largest digital healthcare provider. The company combines physical clinics with a digital platform serving millions of consultations per year across Sweden, Germany, and Norway. The engineering team has around 25 people with startup autonomy inside a profitable international organization with 1,000+ employees and over 1B SEK in revenue.

Doktor.se runs OpenAI and Anthropic models in production across patient and clinician workflows, and that footprint is growing quickly.

What you will do

- Build a deployment platform where teams ship their own services without asking for permission.
- Design infrastructure that is secure by default, including encryption, network isolation, and data residency.
- Create deployment pipelines where security scanning, policy checks, and compliance happen automatically.
- Support and scale infrastructure for AI workloads running models in production.
- Set up observability that gives teams real insight into their own services with useful dashboards and meaningful alerts.
- Work closely with developers to understand what slows them down and fix it by making self-service easier.
- Maintain and evolve cloud account structure, networking, and identity management across multiple regions and products.
- Write infrastructure code, pair with developers to debug slow services, and design isolated infrastructure for new products.
- Make developers faster, not more dependent on the platform team.

What Doktor.se is looking for

- Built and operated Kubernetes in production, including debugging networking issues, designing RBAC policies, and thinking about how multiple teams share a cluster safely.
- Strong AWS knowledge: accounts, networking, IAM, KMS.
- Ability to decide what should live in Kubernetes versus AWS managed services, with a preference for keeping things in Kubernetes where possible.
- Experience with GitOps and infrastructure as code. Flux, ArgoCD, Terraform, or equivalent approaches where infrastructure changes go through pull requests, reviews, and auditable workflows.
- Developer empathy and software engineering background: able to read code, understand pipelines, and discuss architecture meaningfully.
- Security-first mindset: design security into systems instead of reviewing it at the end.
- Strong opinions, with the ability to change them when someone has a better argument.
- University degree in computer science, engineering, or equivalent experience.
